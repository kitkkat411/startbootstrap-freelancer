Swimming at La Jolla Coves 1110 Coast Blvd, La Jolla, CA 92037
Description
One of the best open water swimming spots in Southern California for intermediate to advanced swimmers. La Jolla Cove is a hotspot for swimmers, snorkelers, and kayakers alike with its beautiful views of the San Diego coastline and abundant wildlife. Wearing a wetsuit is recommended but is not required.

Reviews (2)

Mike S.
9/1/14

I had the opportunity to swim two days in early Oct 2008 at the La Jolla Cove. There is a 100 yard wide swimming channel that leads out from the beach towards Scripps Pier (about 2 miles away.) There is a buoy at 1/4 mile out, and 1/2 mile out (there may be one past that spot, but I turned around at the 1/2 mile mark.) On day one, the water was clear (visibility 25+feet), flat, no wind, minimal kelp, lots of small fish near shore, and a perfect experience. On day two, there was some chop and a swell. Visibility was about 12 feet. The water temp both days was near 65 to 68. I wore a wetsuit, but many people did not. There is a lifeguard tower watching you as you swim. If you're a swimmer and enjoy open water, this is mecca on the west coast.

We had our 10 year old grandson along with us on this trip, so this was the perfect spot for us both to enjoy water and sun. There is a small cave area with tide pools for exploring, and you actually swim along side seals (or were they sea lions?). It was fun for all!
