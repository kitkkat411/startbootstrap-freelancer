Iron Mountain Trail Intersection of Hwy 67 and Poway Rd, Poway, CA 92074
Description
This 6.5 mile hike leads you to the foot of the mountain then up and out of the woods until you reach the summit. The ascent itself is fairly intermediate but can still leave you out of breath. The climb pays off with some breathtaking views at the top.

Reviews (2)

Rob B.
9/1/14

This hike is a great workout, and on a clear day offers great views of large areas of the county. The first half offers a long stretch with a moderate grade, then the second half has a greater climb with switchbacks, bringing you to the summit. You go through open areas and wooded areas along the seasonal stream. There is also a spring when you are on the higher elevation, though I would suggest bringing plenty of water. There are plenty of opportunities to view a variety of plants and wildlife of all sorts living in the area. On the summit you can watch hawks circling below you and there's a telescope for you to view many areas of the county and beyond.

My favorite trail in San Diego. Why? 1. The elevation is spread out over enough distance as to give a good workout, but not be over strenuous. This trail is much easier than Woodson in my opinion, though they seem to cover a similar distance. 2. The scenery is quite nice (which isn't common for SD hikes).
